A modular inflatable shape system for interiors, outdoors, art and adaptable living.

What the product is

A soft, portable, reconfigurable object system built from standardized inflatable shapes. It is designed as both a practical product platform and an artistic language of form.

🛋️

Interior use

Lounge seating, floor systems, flexible desks, reading corners, family play zones, and compact workspace layouts.

🏕️

Outdoor use

Temporary seating, vanlife modules, festival installations, glamping furniture, shaded social areas, and lightweight mobile setups.

🎨

Artistic use

Interactive sculpture, gallery objects, modular scenography, tactile environments, branded event spaces, and playful installations.

System breakdown

The product should be developed as a geometry grammar, not as a single object. That makes the platform extensible, manufacturable, and commercially versatile.

Base shapes

Cube, cylinder, wedge, flat panel, sphere, and half-dome. These cover seating, support, surfaces, transitions, and decorative volume.

Docking methods

Magnetic alignment, strap loops, hard click connectors, and optional rail-based structural attachment for larger installations.

Materials

TPU laminated fabric, PVC tarpaulin, ripstop nylon, and drop-stitch panels depending on softness, rigidity, durability, and target market.

Surface identity

Matte monochrome, transparent skins, printed graphics, glow elements, stitched textures, or integrated lighting for premium editions.

Materials and construction strategy

Choose materials based on the balance between softness, rigidity, weather resistance, manufacturing ease, and price.

TPU

Best for premium soft touch, airtight performance, and cleaner aesthetic finishes.

PVC

Lower-cost and durable, suitable for rugged outdoor prototypes and early manufacturing tests.

Drop-stitch

Useful where a flatter, more rigid inflated surface is needed, especially panels and desktops.

Connectors

Begin with loops, Velcro straps, embedded magnets, and 3D-printed PETG hard docks on reinforced patches.

Product development roadmap

The fastest path is to validate geometry and connectors first, then evolve into manufacturing, product families, and brand identity.

1

Define geometry standard

Freeze dimensions, connection zones, valve placement, and stacking logic for the first 4 module types.

2

Build rough prototypes

Test air retention, tactile feel, seam durability, and whether modules remain stable during real use.

3

Pilot use scenarios

Validate home lounge, outdoor kit, event installation, and kids/family use as separate product directions.

4

Small-batch production

Move to improved materials, branded skins, packaging, and a premium visual identity for launch-ready units.

Key questions to answer during prototyping

The best early-stage prototype is the one that reveals what fails, what delights people, and what deserves standardization.

How rigid should the modules feel?

Soft enough to feel inviting, but stable enough to sit on, stack, and dock without collapsing into unusable forms.

Which connector should come first?

Straps and loops are the fastest prototype path. Magnets improve ease of use. Hard click docks become valuable once geometry is fixed.

What is the first commercial story?

A compact personal creative kit is the most understandable launch product, with event and gallery editions following later.

What makes this defensible?

A recognizable geometry system, signature connector logic, branded material finishes, and a clear visual identity across use cases.
